10/2008 - 04/2009 Director of the C++ Development department

Kibitech (Madrid)

I was the director of the C++ development department, and person in charge of a team of 8 engineers. My work consisted of the development of a system of TV2.0 in dedicated hardware, using SpiderMonkey, an in-house SVG renderer, and OpenGL.

The system first interprets a GUI of vectorial graphs, represented in a DOM of about 30,000 SVG nodes. Then it interprets the ECMAscript dynamics (using Mozilla SpiderMonkey), SVG animations, streams of MPEG video (using glib/gstreamer), Bezier shapes (using Cairo), and the other elements that define the graphical appearance (vectorized textures, matrices of cumulative transformations, areas of clipping, masks of opacity, filters of color, effects of blur, etcetera.

The third step consists of turning all the graphical information of the video for the television channels, plus the animated graphics of the menus, the applications, web browser, etcetera, into about 2,000 semitransparent layers that flow from RAM to the GPU of the graphical card. There, it uses a shader programmed in assembler of the graphical processor (ARB and GLSL) to implement the final decompression of the video (YUV-> RGB) in MISP parallelism (600 processing cores), clipping the screen area, blending (alpha pre-multiplied), and the matrices of transformation in color space and ModelView space.

The event handlers were simultaneously translated from the 3D surface areas they belong to, to the 2D actions of the remote control. At the specific task of Picture-in-Picture video streaming with SVG animations, our in-house viewer beat all the major web browsers (this was before WebGL).
